**Post 1 (Hook)**

**Post 2**
The IRS treats crypto as property.

Every trade, swap, or sale is a taxable event.
Yes — swapping $ETH for $SOL on Uniswap is taxable.

Short-term gains (held <1 year): taxed as ordinary income
Long-term gains (held >1 year): 0%, 15%, or 20%

The difference can be thousands of dollars.

**Post 3**
Most people get this wrong:

❌ "I didn't sell, so I don't owe taxes"
→ Wrong if you swapped tokens

❌ "It's on-chain so the IRS can't see it"
→ They can. Centralized exchanges file 1099s.

❌ "DeFi income isn't taxable"
→ Staking rewards, LP fees, and airdrops are all ordinary income.

**Post 4**
The events that *do* trigger taxes:
→ Selling crypto for USD
→ Trading one crypto for another
→ Spending crypto (buying coffee with BTC)
→ Staking rewards received
→ Airdrops received
→ NFT sales

Events that *don't*:
→ Buying crypto with USD
→ Transferring between your own wallets
→ Unrealized gains

**Post 5**
The 6 forms you'll actually use:

📋 Form 8949 — reports each crypto transaction
📋 Schedule D — totals your capital gains/losses
📋 Schedule 1 — staking rewards + airdrops (ordinary income)
📋 Schedule C — if you mined or ran a crypto business
📋 FBAR — if you held >$10K on foreign exchanges
📋 Form 1040 — the main event (check the "digital assets" box)

Missing any of these = audit risk.

**Post 6**
The most underused tax strategy: **tax-loss harvesting**

If you're sitting on unrealized losses:
→ Sell to realize the loss
→ Use it to offset gains from your winners
→ No wash-sale rule for crypto (unlike stocks)
→ Losses carry forward indefinitely

Example: You made $20K on $BTC. You're down $8K on an altcoin. Sell the altcoin → taxable gain drops to $12K.
