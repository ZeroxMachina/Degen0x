**Post 2:**
Pectra = Prague + Electra

Two coordinated upgrades landing at once:

Prague → changes to the execution layer (what runs smart contracts)
Electra → changes to the consensus layer (what keeps the chain secure)

Together, they make Ethereum faster, cheaper, and smarter.

---
**Post 3:**
The biggest change: EIP-7702

Right now, your wallet is dumb. It can only send and receive.

EIP-7702 turns regular wallets into smart accounts — temporarily.

That means:
→ One-click multi-step DeFi transactions
→ Gas fees paid by apps (not you)
→ Batch approvals + swaps in a single click

This is the UX unlock DeFi has needed for years.

---
**Post 4:**
For validators, Pectra brings EIP-7251:

Max effective balance increases from 32 ETH → 2,048 ETH

Big validators no longer need to spin up multiple nodes.

Result: fewer validator nodes, less network overhead, more efficient consensus.

---
**Post 5:**
Blobs are also getting a boost.

EIP-7691 increases the blob target from 3 to 6 per block.

Why does this matter?

Blobs are how L2s (Arbitrum, Base, Optimism) post data to Ethereum cheaply.

More blobs = lower L2 fees = better UX across the entire ecosystem.

---
**Post 6:**
When does Pectra go live?

Estimated: ~April 10, 2026 (pending final testnet confirmation)

What to watch:
→ Smart wallet integrations from Coinbase, MetaMask, Argent
→ L2 fee compression post-launch
→ DeFi protocols updating to use EIP-7702 account abstraction

This isn't hype. The testnets shipped clean.
