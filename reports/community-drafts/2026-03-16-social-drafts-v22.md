## ⚡ THREAD — PayFi Guide (Post Mon Mar 16 — afternoon slot)

```
PayFi is the next DeFi narrative. And almost no one is talking about it correctly. 🧵

1/ PayFi = Payment Finance.

The idea: instead of moving value between parties, you monetize the time-value
of money in transit.

You don't transfer $100. You transfer the yield-generating right to $100
until it's needed. Then settle.

2/ Why this matters in 2026:

→ $150T+ in global payments flow happens annually
→ Each dollar in transit for 2–5 days earns zero yield
→ DeFi protocols can capture that yield during settlement

PayFi is trying to own the settlement layer of global commerce.

3/ Who's building it:

→ Huma Finance: receivables financing on-chain ($500M+ volume)
→ Arf Financial: cross-border USDC settlement for remittance corridors
→ Credix: emerging market credit facilities on Solana
→ Circle + Stripe integration: USDC payment rails in production

4/ The risk:

→ Credit risk: under-collateralized loans default
→ Regulatory: money transmission laws in 160+ jurisdictions
→ Liquidity: PayFi vaults lock capital for weeks or months

Higher yield = real risk. Not free money.

5/ Full guide — what PayFi is, how it works, who's building it, and the risks:

degen0x.com/learn/payfi-guide
```
