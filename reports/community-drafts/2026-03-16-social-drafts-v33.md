→ You set max position sizes, stop losses, and allocation limits
→ The lead trader takes a performance fee (usually 10-20%)

Sounds simple. The details matter enormously.

→ Bybit: largest copy trading market, most diverse strategy pool
→ Bitget: aggressive growth, lowest minimum copy amounts
→ OKX: best for spot copy trading, strong on risk controls

Each has different fee structures, minimum requirements, and trader vetting.

3/ What to look for in a lead trader:

→ 6+ months track record (not just last week's hot streak)
→ Maximum drawdown under 30% (how much they've lost at worst)
→ Consistent returns > big spikes (2-5% monthly beats 50% one month)
→ Copy count trending up (other people trust them)
→ Transparent about strategy (are they leveraged? which pairs?)

The leaderboards show lifetime P&L. The drawdown history tells the real story.

4/ What to avoid:

→ Traders with < 3 months history
→ 100x leverage strategies (one bad trade = liquidation)
→ "Never lost" records (they haven't been through a real crash)
→ Traders who don't share their strategy publicly
→ Platforms without clear fee disclosures

Copy trading can work. Blind copying always fails eventually.
