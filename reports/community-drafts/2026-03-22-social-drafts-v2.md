THREAD: The SEC's 5-Category Token Taxonomy — explained in 60 seconds 🧵

1/ The SEC and CFTC just published a 68-page joint interpretation classifying crypto into 5 categories. This is the biggest regulatory clarity moment since the Howey Test debates. Here's the breakdown:

2/ The 5 categories:
→ Digital Commodities (BTC, ETH, SOL + 13 more)
→ Digital Collectibles (NFTs with no profit expectation)
→ Digital Tools (utility tokens)
→ Stablecoins (pegged assets)
→ Digital Securities (tokenized traditional securities)

3/ Why it matters: Only Digital Securities are subject to securities law. The other 4 categories have clearer, lighter regulation. Staking? Not a security. Mining? Not a security. Airdrops (no consideration)? Not a security.

4/ The 16 named digital commodities: $BTC $ETH $SOL $XRP $ADA $LINK $AVAX $DOT $XLM $HBAR $LTC $DOGE $SHIB $XTZ $BCH $APT $ALGO

If you hold any of these — your regulatory risk just dropped significantly.

5/ Caveat: This is an interpretive release, not law. The CLARITY Act is still pending in Congress. But this is the strongest signal yet that the "everything is a security" era is over.
