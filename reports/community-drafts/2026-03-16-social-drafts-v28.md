```
You stake $ETH to secure Ethereum. Cool.

Restaking lets you re-use that same staked ETH to secure OTHER networks and services simultaneously.

One deposit. Multiple yield sources. More security for everyone.

AVS are the services that restaked ETH secures:

3/ The ecosystem is exploding:

→ EigenLayer: The restaking protocol ($15B+ TVL)

→ Concentration risk (too much ETH in one protocol)

Higher yield = higher risk. Always.
```

## SUNDAY APR 6 — Intent-Based DEX Trading

### Twitter Thread — Stop Getting Sandwiched: Intent-Based Trading (5 posts)

```
Thread: Every time you swap on a DEX, MEV bots are probably taking money from you.

Intent-based trading fixes this. Here's how — and which protocols do it best. 🧵

1/ The MEV problem:

When you submit a swap on Uniswap:
→ Your transaction sits in the mempool
→ MEV bots see it before it executes
→ They front-run your trade (buy before you, sell after)
→ You get a worse price. They profit.

This costs DEX users billions per year.

2/ Intent-based trading flips the model:

Instead of submitting a transaction, you submit an INTENT:
"I want to swap 1 ETH for USDC at the best available price."

Solvers (specialized market makers) compete to fill your order.
The best price wins. No mempool exposure. No sandwich attacks.

3/ Who's building this:

→ CoW Protocol: Pioneered intent-based trading (CoW Swap)
→ UniswapX: Uniswap's intent-based routing layer
→ 1inch Fusion: Solver-based execution for 1inch
→ Flashbots SUAVE: Infrastructure for intent settlement

These aren't small projects. This is where the biggest DEX teams are building.

4/ Why you should care:

→ Better prices (solvers compete for your order)
→ No MEV extraction (your trade isn't visible to bots)
→ Gas optimization (solvers batch transactions)
→ Cross-chain potential (intents can route across chains)

The UX is the same. The execution is dramatically better.
```
