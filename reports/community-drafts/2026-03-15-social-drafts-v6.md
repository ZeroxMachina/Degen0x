**Post 2**
Why is Bitcoin vulnerable?

Bitcoin uses ECDSA (Elliptic Curve Digital Signature Algorithm) to secure wallets.

A sufficiently powerful quantum computer can run Shor's algorithm to reverse-engineer private keys from public keys.

~4M BTC in "exposed" addresses (public key visible) = ~$290B at risk.

**Post 3**
How close are we?

Timeline estimates vary wildly:

🟡 Conservative: 20-40 years
🟠 Mid-range: 10-15 years
🔴 Aggressive (DARPA/NIST): 5-7 years

The catch: nobody knows when the "cryptographically relevant quantum computer" (CRQC) arrives.

But NIST and Europol both say: **start migrating now**.

**Post 4**
NIST just made it official.

In 2024-2025, NIST finalized three post-quantum cryptography standards:
→ ML-KEM (key exchange)
→ ML-DSA (digital signatures)
→ SLH-DSA (hash-based signatures)

These use lattice-based and hash-based math that quantum computers can't efficiently break.

**Post 5**
Which crypto is already quantum-safe?

🛡️ **QRL (Quantum Resistant Ledger)** — lattice signatures (XMSS) built-in since genesis
🛡️ **Abelian** — lattice-based, zk-privacy + quantum resistance combo
🛡️ **QANplatform** — NIST-standard CRYSTALS-Dilithium signatures

**Post 6**
What about Bitcoin and Ethereum?

Both have quantum migration on the roadmap:

→ Bitcoin: BIP-360 (P2QRH) proposal for quantum-safe addresses — not yet merged
→ Ethereum: Vitalik's roadmap includes post-quantum via zk-STARKs + account abstraction

Timeline: years of testing before mainnet. The window isn't closed — but it's narrowing.

**Post 7**

1️⃣ Move BTC to a fresh address — reduces public key exposure
